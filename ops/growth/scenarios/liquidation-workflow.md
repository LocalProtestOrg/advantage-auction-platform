## How Commercial Liquidation Works on Advantage

The process mirrors the estate auction flow with commercial-specific differences:

**Step 1 — Inventory assessment**
Describe what you have, rough quantity, and location.
Large-lot assessments may require an on-site review.

**Step 2 — Auction planning**
We determine lot structure (individual items vs. bulk lots vs. lot bundles).
Commercial auctions may have different lot minimums and buyer pickup requirements.

**Step 3 — Listing and photography**
Our team photographs and lists each lot. For large inventories, we can work with
your team to create a joint inventory manifest.

**Step 4 — Auction runs**
Online bidding. Business buyers, resellers, and individual buyers all bid.
Commercial lots typically attract stronger bids when shipping is available.

**Step 5 — Pickup / logistics**
Buyers retrieve items during scheduled pickup windows.
For large equipment or freight items, we coordinate with buyers on logistics.
[Shipping options subject to admin enablement — see seller capabilities]

**Step 6 — Payout**
Proceeds distributed after buyer payment clears.

## Differentiation from Estate Sales

| Factor | Estate Sale | Commercial Liquidation |
|---|---|---|
| Item types | Household goods, antiques, art | Equipment, inventory, fixtures |
| Buyer type | General public, collectors | Businesses, resellers, contractors |
| Lot structure | Individual items | Mix of individual + bulk |
| Timeline urgency | Moderate | Often high (lease deadline, closure date) |
| Shipping | Varies by lot | Often required for equipment |
